Penetration Testing

Estimated Engagement Period: 1-4 weeks.

A full-scale sophisticated cyber attack simulation with specific targets in mind, with a lot of communication and a detailed report.

It includes a cyclical process of Scan, Exploit, Repeat: Attempt to apply all potential vulnerabilities found to gain further intel/access, the process gets repeated until no further exploits can be applied.

Since some public exploits have the risk of bringing down the service, any risky scripts will be checked with your IT Manager prior to running them. This process involves regular communication with your IT department.

Applicable Areas Include: Internal, External, Web, Mobile, Wi-Fi, Physical, Cloud, and Hardware.

Red Team Engagement

Estimated Engagement Period: 1-2 months or long term ongoing engagement.

This process is a bit similar to penetration testing, with some key differences. For example, your organization will need dedicated "blue team" ready against any and all potential attacks from this exercise.

The attack will be more general instead of having specific targets, and the attack timing, targets will not be communicated prior, simulating real and unexpected attacks to see how your organization reacts and respondes to them.

This process can be a short term engagement, as well as long term. You can verify your incident response process and find additional insight on how to improve them.

Phishing Campaign

Estimated Engagement Period: 1-8 weeks.

This is essentially a test for the weakest link in cyber security, the people. How well does your organization hold up against a sophisticated phishing campaign?

We can offer various levels of difficulty, phishing attempts can go from a poorly drafted email trying to give you "free" money, to highly convincing with prior information gathering through social media and other sources.

Get real statistics and advice, find out how many attempts were successful, and additional advices, and possibly training on what to look out for in order to avoid them, as well as improving your social media policy.
